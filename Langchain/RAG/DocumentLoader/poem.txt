Friendship is not loud—
it doesn’t knock or demand to be seen.
It slips in quietly,
like sunlight through half-open curtains,
warming corners you didn’t know were cold.

It’s in the pauses between words,
where silence feels safe, not empty.
In laughter that spills too freely,
over nothing and everything at once.

A friend is the one who stays
when the story gets messy—
who reads the unfinished pages of you
and doesn’t try to rewrite them.

They are the steady rhythm
when your thoughts run wild,
the soft “I’m here”
when the world feels too much.

Not every day is bright,
not every moment easy—
but friendship is the quiet promise
that you won’t have to face it alone.

And somehow, in their presence,
even the ordinary feels like
something worth holding onto.
